# Winterizing Pipes Las Vegas: Why a Desert City Still Busts Lines on Freeze Nights
Vegas gets those sneaky cold snaps—freeze risk is real—so the smart play is a **whole-home freeze protection package**, not a last-minute faucet drip. The goal is simple: keep vulnerable lines from freezing, expanding, and splitting.

Las Vegas sees freezing temps with some regularity, and the damage usually hits **garage walls, exterior hose bibs, backflow devices, attic runs, and cabinets on outside walls**. The local soil profile (loamy, carbonatic soils with a petrocalcic “caliche” layer) matters because it often drives **shallow/exposed routing** for irrigation and exterior plumbing components—and those are the first to get nailed when the wind chills the wall. Seasonal behavior is predictable: the first real cold week is when we see surprise failures, and then the **next day’s thaw** is when leaks show up. Material stress is straight physics—water expands as it freezes—so prevention is about **insulation, sealing drafts, controlled heat, and smart shutoffs**.

In Vegas, it’s not the “cold season” that gets you—it’s the **one or two nights** you didn’t expect. Get the weak points handled once, and you’re not dealing with surprise drywall cuts and cabinet floods the next morning.

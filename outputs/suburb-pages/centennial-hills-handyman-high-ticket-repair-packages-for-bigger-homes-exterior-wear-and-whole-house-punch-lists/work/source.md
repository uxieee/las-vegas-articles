# Centennial Hills Handyman: High-Ticket Repair Packages for Bigger Homes, Exterior Wear, and Whole-House Punch Lists
In Centennial Hills, the bigger handyman jobs usually come from **space, sun, and scope**—larger homes, longer exterior runs, and more little issues stacking into one real project. We see this a lot up there: what starts as “a few fixes” usually turns into a higher-ticket package once the doors, walls, trim, gates, and water-control details all get looked at together.

Centennial Hills is positioned as a spacious, family-friendly northwest Las Vegas community with newer housing and room to spread out, and that changes the repair pattern a bit. Instead of one tiny isolated problem, we usually see **whole-house punch-list work**: upstairs drywall seams showing in hallway light, exterior trim opening on the hot side, garage-to-house doors drifting, and gates getting fussy after a windy stretch. Underneath, the valley’s native soils are **shallow, well-drained alluvial soils with carbonates/caliche**, so small movement can still telegraph as latch misalignment, recurring cracks, and fence drift even in newer homes. Then the climate does what Vegas always does—**extreme heat, strong sun, and monsoon-season flash flooding** stress sealants, hardware, coatings, and drainage details all at once. Around Centennial Hills, the durable fix is usually a **bundled high-ticket scope**: tighten the openings, reinforce the finishes, correct the water path, and clean up the exterior details so the house feels maintained instead of patched in pieces.

# Las Vegas Handyman Cost Guide (2026): What High-Ticket Projects Really Cost in the Valley
Bigger Las Vegas jobs price up because **heat timing, access, and hidden scope** (water paths, movement, sun damage) turn “one task” into a package. Most crews will quote these as **project totals or day rates**, not a tiny hourly number.

Out here, the ground is often **shallow, well-drained, loamy, carbonatic soil with a petrocalcic (caliche) layer**, which is why we see small seasonal movement show up as **door alignment drift, hairline cracking, and fence/gate racking**—and those “small” symptoms stack into bigger scopes fast. Vegas weather is the other multiplier: **triple-digit heat and UV** cook caulk/paint and stress materials daily, and **monsoon storms can cause flash flooding** that exposes drainage and moisture issues in one shot. Seasonally, the clean way to keep a high-ticket job from turning into rework is planning around material behavior: hot surfaces, flash-dry coatings, cooled nights, pull joints, and anything exterior needs prep that matches that cycle. Most supplies are easy to grab same-day locally (fasteners, sealants, hangers, setting compounds), but **matching textures/finishes cleanly** is what usually adds labor and revisits.

| High-ticket handyman-style packages (Las Vegas) | What it usually includes | Typical total |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **Pre-listing “whole-house punch list” (1–2 days)** | drywall touch-ups, door tuning, caulk resets, misc hardware | **$1,800–$5,500** |
| **Full rental turnover refresh** | patch/texture blend, trim reset, fixture swaps, function checks | **$1,500–$6,000** |
| **Exterior trim/wood repair bundle** | multiple rot sections, rebuild/prime/seal, water-path corrections | **$2,000–$7,500** |
| **Multi-room drywall + texture matching** | wide floats, corner reinforcement, blend across sightlines | **$1,500–$6,500** |
| **Deck clean/prep/stain (medium size)** | wash/brighten, sand hotspots, stain timed to temps | **$1,200–$4,500** |
| **Fence section rebuild + gate rebuild/tune** | posts/rails as needed, hardware reset, latch alignment | **$1,500–$6,000** |

In Vegas, high-ticket work stays predictable when it’s scoped by **systems** (water control, alignment, sealing, finish matching) instead of a random list of tiny tasks. The heat and monsoon don’t care what your budget was—materials will do what physics says they’ll do.

# Summerlin North Handyman: High-Ticket Home Repair for Doors, Walls, Trim, and Exterior Wear
In Summerlin North, the bigger jobs are usually the ones where **movement, heat, and water control** all tie together. We see a lot of “small symptoms” up here turn into premium-scope projects because the houses are tight, the finishes are visible, and the desert does not play nice.

Summerlin North sits in **northwest Las Vegas, north of Summerlin Parkway**, inside the broader Summerlin master-planned area, so the homes tend to show wear in a more obvious way: a door that drifts, a ceiling seam that telegraphs in afternoon light, exterior trim that opens up on the sunny side. The valley’s native soils are **shallow, well-drained alluvial soils with carbonates/caliche**, which is why tiny movement can show up as latch misalignment, recurring drywall cracks, or fence and gate drift even when nothing looks “seriously wrong.” Then Summerlin’s climate piles on: **extreme heat, strong sun, and monsoon-season flash flooding** stress caulk lines, coatings, fasteners, and drainage details all at once. Seasonal behavior is pretty predictable around here—summer heat makes materials expand and dry out, then a storm day exposes every weak seam and splash-back point. We handle these as **high-ticket repair packages** instead of random one-offs: align the opening, reinforce the finish, correct the water path, and get the exterior sealed back up so the fix actually sticks.

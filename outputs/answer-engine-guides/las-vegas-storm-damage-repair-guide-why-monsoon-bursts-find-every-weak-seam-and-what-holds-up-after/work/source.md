# Las Vegas Storm Damage Repair Guide: Why Monsoon Bursts Find Every Weak Seam (and What Holds Up After)
Vegas storms don’t usually “soak you for days” — they hit hard, fast, and sideways, and that’s when weak flashing, loose siding, and tired caulk get exposed. We treat storm damage like a **system problem** (water path + wind load), not a bunch of random patches.

In Las Vegas, a big monsoon dump can turn into **sheet runoff** because we’ve got **loamy alluvial soils with caliche layers** that don’t always absorb water evenly, so splash-back and pooling show up right at the slab edge and lower walls. Wind-driven rain will push water into tiny gaps around trim, storm doors, and siding seams, and once it’s behind the finish, the **heat** bakes it and makes caulk fail even faster. Seasonal behavior matters: late-summer storms expose the weak points, then fall/winter temp swings make the “fixed” spots telegraph again if the repair wasn’t reinforced. Material stress is predictable here—fasteners loosen, corner joints open, gutter corners leak, and exterior paint films crack under UV. The best high-ticket repairs bundle the full path: **exterior sealing/flashing corrections, drainage/discharge fixes, damaged material replacement, then interior patch/prime so stains don’t ghost back through**—and most of the parts are easy to source locally (hangers, sealants, flashing, trim stock), it’s the sequencing that makes it last.

| High-ticket storm repair scope | What it usually includes | Typical total |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| Water intrusion diagnosis + targeted exterior corrections | Trace path, re-seal/flash key joints, test flow | $750–$2,500 |
| Gutter/discharge overhaul (problem corners) | Clean/flush, reseal corners, add extensions, tune pitch | $600–$3,000 |
| Exterior trim/fascia/soffit rebuild (localized) | Replace damaged sections, prime/seal all faces, re-caulk | $1,500–$6,500 |
| Siding/seam + corner remediation | Re-secure, replace damaged panels, seal joints, blend finish | $1,200–$5,500 |
| Interior water damage reset (drywall/texture/paint-ready) | Cut-out/patch as needed, stain-block prime, blend texture | $900–$4,500 |
| Wind damage package (fence/gate + hardware) | Straighten/rebuild sections, reset posts as needed, tune gate | $1,000–$6,000 |

Around here, the most expensive “storm damage” is the stuff that gets patched cosmetically while the water path stays open. If you fix the **entry point + discharge** first, the interior repairs stop being a recurring bill every monsoon season.

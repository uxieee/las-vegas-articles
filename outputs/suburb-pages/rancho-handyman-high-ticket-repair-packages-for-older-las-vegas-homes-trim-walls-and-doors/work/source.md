# Rancho Handyman: High-Ticket Repair Packages for Older Las Vegas Homes, Trim, Walls, and Doors
In Rancho, the bigger jobs usually come from **older housing stock meeting desert wear**—one sticking door, one wall crack, one ugly exterior seam, and suddenly it’s a whole-house scope. The neighborhood is known for **historic homes, mid-century design, and strong residential character**, so the repairs need to look clean, not just “fixed.”

Rancho is a **central-west Las Vegas** area near **Rancho Drive**, and that older-home profile changes the repair pattern right away: more trim movement, more wall-finish inconsistency, more doors that have drifted a little over time, and more exterior details that show their age in hard sunlight. The valley’s native soils are **shallow, well-drained alluvial soils with carbonate-rich caliche**, which is one reason small movement can telegraph as latch misalignment, recurring cracks, and fence drift even when the house feels basically sound. Then Las Vegas climate piles on: **extreme summer heat, strong sun, and monsoon-season flash flooding** stress sealants, coatings, fasteners, and drainage details all at once. Around Rancho, the durable fix is usually a **high-ticket bundled scope**—align the openings, reinforce the finish work, rebuild the tired trim, and correct the water path so the same symptom doesn’t come right back after the next hot stretch.

Rancho homes have character, but character also means the shortcuts show fast—especially in hallway light or on a sun-baked exterior wall. When we group the work by **movement, finish quality, and water control**, the house feels maintained instead of pieced together.
